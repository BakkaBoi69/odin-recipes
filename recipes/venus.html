Capezzoli di Venere (Nipples of Venus)

Description

Capezzoli di Venere, also known as "Nipples of Venus" is a traditional Italian recipe that is made with small pasta shells filled with a mixture of ricotta cheese, spinach and parmesan. It's a dish that is sure to please both vegetarians and meat-lovers alike. The name of the dish is playful and suggestive, making it perfect for a romantic dinner. But, let's be real, the real reason why Capezzoli di Venere is so popular is because of its sensual shape and creamy filling. It's the pasta that says, "I'm not just any ordinary pasta, I'm a Capezzoli di Venere, and I'm here to seduce your taste buds." It's the perfect dish to make for a special occasion or a romantic night in.

When making Capezzoli di Venere, it is important to pay attention to the cooking time as they can easily go from sensual to burnt. A little burnt on the edges can add a nice smoky flavor, but be careful not to burn them or you'll end up with "Capezzoli di Inferno" (hell's nipples). This dish is a great way to spice up a dinner with your loved one, or a fun way to impress your friends and family. With its playful name, delicious taste and beautiful presentation, Capezzoli di Venere is sure to be a hit.

Ingredients:

  • Small pasta shells
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Spinach
  • Parmesan
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic (optional)
  • Basil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta shells according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, sauté garlic in olive oil over medium heat. Add spinach and cook until wilted.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, parmesan, and spinach mixture. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Stuff the pasta shells with the ricotta mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  5. Bake at 350°F for 20-25 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.
